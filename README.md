## 1. Eligibility

- Open to **+2 (Grade 11–12)** and **undergraduate students** enrolled in any recognized institution in Nepal.
- A valid student ID must be presented during check-in.
- Each team must have a **minimum of 2** and a **maximum of 5** members.
- **Solo participation is not allowed.**
- A participant may only be a member of **one team**. Registering with multiple teams will result in disqualification.
- Students from any discipline (engineering, management, humanities, science, etc.) are eligible.

## 3. Event Format and Rules

- The hackathon runs for **2 days**: 13–14 Ashadh 2083 at Nepal Engineering College, Bhaktapur.
- The theme is **technology and tourism**, focused on real-world challenges in Bagmati Province.
- **All projects must be built during the hackathon period.** Pre-built or substantially pre-started projects are not allowed.
- Open-source libraries, APIs, and public datasets are allowed. Proper attribution is required.
- AI coding tools (e.g., GitHub Copilot, ChatGPT) may be used as aids only — not to generate the core solution. Judges may ask teams to explain any part of their submission.
- Participants must bring their own **laptops and hardware**. The venue provides electricity and internet.
- All work must be **original**. Plagiarism results in immediate disqualification.

## 6. Judging Criteria

Projects will be scored by a panel of judges across five criteria:

| Criteria | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Innovation & Creativity | Originality of the idea and approach |
| Technical Execution | Quality of build, code, and implementation |
| Relevance to Tourism | How well it addresses a real tourism challenge in Bagmati Province |
